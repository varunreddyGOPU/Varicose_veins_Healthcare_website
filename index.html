Why is it important to Know
about Varicose veins

  1. 01

    Varicose veins are a common condition affecting the lower extremities, with a complex underlying pathophysiology including genetic predisposition, weakened vascular walls, and increased intravenous pressure. There is a range of conservative treatment options available, including external compression, lifestyle modifications, and medical therapy.

  2. 02

    Varicose veins are a common medical condition that affects a large portion of the population. According to studies, approximately 23% of US adults suffer from this disease, with over 40 million people affected in the United States alone. It is a hereditary condition, with a family history being a significant risk factor. If both parents have varicose veins, the chances of developing the disease are close to 90%. Even if one parent is affected, daughters have a 60% chance, while sons have a 25% chance of developing the disease. Women are at a higher risk of developing varicose veins, with a prevalence of 55%, compared to men at 45%.

  3. 03

    As individuals age, the risk of developing varicose veins increases significantly. It is estimated that 41% of women over the age of 50 have varicose veins, and over 50% of the US population over 50 years old has the disease. Weight also plays a role in the development of varicose veins, with overweight women having a higher risk of developing the disease.

  4. 04

    Varicose veins can be more common in some persons due to things like age, family history, gender, obesity, pregnancy, constipation, and prolonged standing. The symptoms, which get worse with extended standing, include a heavy, achy feeling, itching, and a burning sensation.
